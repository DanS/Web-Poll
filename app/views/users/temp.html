Transforming Enemy Images

KEYS:

a. Every human action, however abhorrent to us, is an expression of feelings and needs just like our own.

b. Our own reactions to others' actions are also expressions of feelings and needs.

c. We tend to have “enemy” images of others when our needs are critically unmet and when we don’t see the human expression in the other person’s actions.

d. Connecting to another person with empathy humanizes them to us and makes it more possible to create connection to meet both our needs.

1. Identify a person whose behavior you would like to see change, and about whom you have strong judgments. Describe the situation in 40 words or less. (You can also choose a group if you want.)




2. Write down your judgments of this person.




3. Translate your judgments into observations, feelings, and needs.




4. Think of your observation of the person’s behavior, and write down three guesses as to what might be the feelings and needs motivating this behavior.

    a. Is this person feeling ___________________ because they need ___________________ ?

    b. Is this person feeling ___________________ because they need ___________________ ?

    c. Is this person feeling ___________________ because they need ___________________ ?

5. Think of three requests you might make of this person coming out of an intention of meeting both your needs and theirs.
